What to do in Bangkok

สิ่งที่ต้องทำในกรุงเทพ

Bangkok, Thailand’s capital city, is Saint’s hometown. A sprawling cosmopolitan city, you can get a taste of everything here -- from temples to thai massage, shopping to street food, floating markets to fine dining, night markets to nightlife. There are enough attractions to fill a few days (and many day trips you can take to cities outside of Bangkok), but we recommend spending even more time here if you’re a shopaholic. Bangkok is infamous for its traffic jams, so plan your daily itineraries carefully to minimize time spent getting around.

Must Do

  • Thai Massage | Thai Massage parlours are everywhere, and a 60 minute massage usually costs less than $10USD.
  • Eat | All the foods

Sightseeing

  • Grand Palace & Temple of the Emerald Buddha (Wat Phra Kaew), and Temple of Reclining Buddha (Wat Pho) | These are Bangkok’s must-see attractions. All three sites are located together. Be sure when visiting any royal site or temple to prepare for the strict dress code (no tank tops, and no shorts. As Nic has learned, leggings aren’t considered pants.)
  • Khlong Lat Mayom Floating Market | The bigger floating markets are located a few hours outside Bangkok
  • Koh Kred Island | TBD
  • Chatuchak/Jatujak Market | Massive day market only open on weekends. Very tourist-friendly, but locals shop here too. They sell a little bit of everything. Check out Nic’s favorite section -- the exotic pets and animal section.

Shopping

  • Pratunam Platinum | A shopper’s paradise, best for women’s apparel. Great food court on the top floor. Most shops are open between 12-5pm.
  • MBK (Maboonkrong) | A gadget geek’s paradise, best for electronics.
  • Siam Square | The shopping center of the city, with many high-end shopping destinations (e.g. Siam Paragon) interspersed with local shopping and great food at restaurants and in mall food courts.

Eating

  • Chinatown (Yaowaraj) | Great street food, just make sure the stalls look clean.
  • Icon Siam | The ground floor of this flashy new mega-mall has a simulated floating market, and a huge assortment of local food and desserts from all parts of Thailand.
  • [Railroad] Night Market | TBD
