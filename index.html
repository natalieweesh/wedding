Where to Stay

อยู่ที่ไหน

We recommend you stay close to public transportation (BTS Skytrain and/or MRT Subway), which can take you to most of the major tourist attractions and help you avoid the infamous Bangkok traffic jams. We recommend staying either in the Sukhumvit area or the Siam area. Book your hotel on Agoda.com for the best prices.

Sukhumvit Area (BTS Asoke Station / MRT Sukhumvit Station):

The most central and convenient location with both BTS and MRT stations, but heavy car traffic at all times. The landmark for this area (which you tell any taxi driver) is the Terminal 21 shopping mall with great restaurants and a cheap food court. During the day, Sukhumvit has many massage parlours and local street food if you walk through the sois (“soi” = alley/small street). At night, there’s plenty of clubs and bars in Soi 11. Depending on your interests, you may want to explore or avoid the redlight districts in Nana Plaza (Soi 4) and Soi Cowboy (Soi 23).

Siam Area (BTS Siam / Chidlom / Ratchathewi Station)

The most central location for local students because of its diversity of shopping options. Siam BTS station is the busiest BTS stop and you’ll have to wait in line during rush hour. The most famous malls are Siam Paragon for high-end shopping (great food court on the ground floor), MBK center for electronics, and Pratunam Platinum Fashion Mall for cheap fashion (most shops open from 12PM-6PM, great food court at Level 6). The Siam area across from Siam Paragon has a lot of shopping streets and restaurants. Our favorite places are TGIF Market (evening/night market in front of Central World with food and fashion), Siam Pandan (best pandan traditional thai pancake, Soi 9), Napar Massage (should call and make an appointment), Kluay Kluay (Banana Banana) for fun banana desserts, and Inter Thai Food (great authentic Thai food).

Travel Tips

อยู่ที่ไหน
  • Grab Taxi: Download the Grab app (it’s like Uber) and add your credit card. You’ll need Grab to get around when there are no taxis easily available.
  • Sim Card: Buy a sim card at the airport with unlimited internet and top up 100 baht (prepaid minutes). We like to get ours from the green AIS stall near baggage claim #10.
  • Cash: Thailand is still a cash economy. Be sure to withdraw some cash at the airport ATM.
  • Public Transportation: The skytrain and subway are great ways to get around, especially to avoid rush hour street traffic. Note that the MRT closes vaguely between 10-10:30pm, and sometimes the security guard won’t let you in close to that time. Avoid public buses as they are unreliable.
  • Weather: Winter in Thailand is still quite hot, so plan appropriately. Note that temples and destinations owned by the royal family have dress codes (no tank tops, no shorts) so you’ll either have to dress appropriately, bring a sarong or scarf to cover up, or be prepared to rent or buy some apparel on the spot.
  • Personal Tour Guides: Although we would very much like to be your personal tour guides, we sadly won’t have time to attend to everyone. As a substitute, consider hiring a local guide from TakeMeTour to take you on a private local experience. TakeMeTour is a startup based in Bangkok and Saint knows the co-founders so we can attest to its legitimacy.
