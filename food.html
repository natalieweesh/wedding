Savory Food

อาหารคาว

Boat Noodles (Guay Tiaw Ruer) | Dark herbal noodle soup with pork or beef

Tom Yum Soup (Tom Yum Goong) | Spicy sour soup, usually with shrimp

Khao Mun Gai | Thail-style chicken rice, comes with clear winter melon soup. Great with fermented soybean/garlic/chili sauce. You can add liver and congealed blood as “toppings”

Hoy Tod | Fried mussel or oyster pancake; usually sold alongside Pad Thai

Green Curry (Gang Kiew Wan) | Spicy coconut milk curry with bamboo shoots and small Thai

Drunken Noodle (Pad Kee Mao) | Spicy basil stir-fried broad rice noodles

Som Tum (Papaya Salad) | Spicy raw papaya salad from Northeastern Thailand. Have it with sticky rice (Kao Niew)

Larb | Spicy minced meat lime salad with a side of raw veggies, also from Northeastern Thailand. Have it with sticky rice (Kao Niew)

Leng | Super spicy herbal pork bone in clear soup with heaps of green chilies

Yen Ta Fo | Tomato-based pink colored rice noodle soup, non-spicy

Fruits

ผลไม้

Chompoo (Rose Apple) | One of Nic’s favourite fruits, chompoo is crunchy, juicy, and not too sweet. You can buy it on the street sliced or bite into the whole fruit like an apple

Ma-Nuang (Mango) | Thailand has many types of mangoes ranging from ripe and sweet (often sold with optional sticky rice) to green and sour

Ma-Prow (Coconut) | Fresh coconut water is everywhere. Ask for a spoon to scrape the flesh out after you drink!

Too-Rian (Durian) | Try the “king of fruits” if you dare

Mang Kood (Mangosteen) | Another one of Nic’s favourite fruits, but hard to describe. It has the texture of a peach, but its white flesh is hidden beneath a thick purple skin. To open, squeeze it until the skin cracks open

Sao-Wa-Rod (Passion Fruit) | One of Saint’s favorite fruits, pure passion fruit is sour and full of crunchy edible seeds. Often served as a drink. If you like super sour things, find a stall that sells it without added sugar

Gaew Mun Gorn (Dragon Fruit) | Mildly sweet fruit with tons of tiny edible black seeds. One of Nic’s favorites

Farang (Guava) | Super crunchy, dense, pear-like fruit. Avoid its little hard seeds

Ma-La-Kor (Papaya) | Unlike the unripened crunchy green papayas in papaya salad, ripe papayas are sweet (and a good laxative if needed)

Sa-Pa-Rod (Pineapple) | There are many types of pineapples sold on the street. The ones sliced in a fan-shape are more full-flavored but the cute little mini ones are extra crunchy and sweet

Kanoon (Jackfruit) | One of the world’s largest fruits. Its bright yellow flesh is sweet with a unique flavor similar to a mango, banana, and pineapple
